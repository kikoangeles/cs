The Roaring Twenties (1920s)


The roaring twenties brought about a sense of playfulness and freedom, and this was reflected in the media and films that were produced in the era.
Up until that moment, film wasn’t a creative medium: filmmakers just stuck to the simple point and shoot method of filming. However, a string of films that challenged this notion
came into the scene. We see this in films like the German Expressionist classic, “Nosferatu”, which makes use of shadows and experimental lighting. Or the classic “Passion of Joan
of Arc”, which uses up-close shots. Though this method of filming seems like the norm now, it was revolutionary for its time, and it foreshadowed the million different ways one can
film now.
